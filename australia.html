Chapter 01

Working in Gold Mines

My first months in Australia were nothing like I imagined. I headed to Western Australia to work in the gold mines — a decision driven by curiosity, the need to fund my stay, and the desire to experience something truly different.

The Outback is brutal and beautiful at the same time. Temperatures regularly exceeded 45°C, shifts started before sunrise, and the work was relentlessly physical. There was no room for shortcuts — safety protocols were strict and every person on site relied on each other.

I worked alongside people from all over the world — Australians, Filipinos, South Africans, New Zealanders — which turned every day into a small cultural lesson in itself.

Chapter 02

Internship at Knowz — Surfer Paradise

Add Knowz photo
Add photo
Add photo

After the mines, I moved to the Est-Coast and joined Knowz, an AI startup building tools that help people find and understand information faster. It was a complete change of environment: open office, sprints, product meetings, and a team that moved fast.

During my 3-month internship, I took ownership of an innovative feature: an AI assistant that automatically generates no-code workflows from simple user instructions. This required diving deep into prompt engineering—translating business logic into effective prompts and guiding the AI's step-by-step reasoning.

Working closely with the CTO, Ignacio Diaz, and my colleague Taïna Delhaye, I learned how to ship quickly, deal with ambiguity, and bridge the gap between complex AI behavior and user-friendly product design. The entire codebase and all communication was in English, which dramatically accelerated my technical fluency.

What I worked on

  • Designed a 5-state finite state machine with contextual memory for the AI assistant
  • Built an automatic JSON block generator to power the no-code interface
  • Engineered complex prompts to simplify business logic without losing meaning
  • Conducted product discussions, testing, and use-case analysis directly with the CTO
  • Wrote technical documentation and communicated entirely in English

Year at a Glance

How the 12 months unfolded.

Month 1–2

Arrival & Setup

Landed in Perth. Sorted housing, opened a bank account, buying a car, and started navigated bureaucracy entirely in English. First shock of total immersion.

Month 2–6

Gold Mine Work — Outback

Headed inland to work on a mine site. Physically demanding, mentally rewarding. Saved enough to fund the rest of the year.

Month 6–7

Exploration & Job Hunt

Traveled along the East Coast — Melbourne, Brisbane, Sydney. Started applying for tech internships.

Month 7–12

Internship at Knowz — Sydney

Joined the Knowz team. Worked as a software engineering intern in a fast-paced AI startup environment until the end of my visa.

What I Took Away

English Fluency

Living entirely in English for a year brought my level to a confident professional standard — both written and spoken.

Resilience

Going from extreme physical labour to a tech internship forced me to adapt constantly. I became comfortable in discomfort.

Full Autonomy

Managing visa, finances, housing, and career across the globe with no safety net built real independence.

Cultural Intelligence

Working with dozens of nationalities in two very different environments sharpened my cross-cultural communication.

Startup Mindset

Knowz taught me to move fast, own my work, and ship without waiting for perfect conditions.

Life Perspective

A year away changed how I see work, ambition, and what matters. Some lessons only come from doing things the hard way.
